Testing of the website

Administrative details

The name of the fictional client is Andres' Bananas, and is the sole proprietor of Andres' Bananas foodtruck

Tester audience

Seeing that our intended audience is for students studying at Gløshaugen who need a little food. We went in the corridors and asked students if they had some time to spare to test out our webpage for a food ordering system.

Testing platforms

The testing platforms depended on situation, we had published the page on folk.ntnu and gave the link to students after asking if they would be willing to test out our page. This resulted in different operative systems(macOS and Windows) on different screensizes. The browsers were primaraly safari and google chrome in these cases. We also brought our own laptops and asked other students to test the website on those, where we had opened the page in opera and firefox.

We had brought our own laptops, so that the students wouldn't have to hassle with getting up their own.
The platforms we tested on where a windows 16.8 inch, and a macbook 13inch in different browsers like chrome, opera, firefox and safari

Self-reflection

Overall we had a very good experience working on this project. We met up and worked together on mondays and tuesdays. When not working together, we used git-hub so that we were able to work by ourselves in different places. Du to our usergroup being collegestudents, we mostly tested on collegestudents. Collegestudents are usually good with tech and therefore we could have for example tested on elderly people as well, to ensure that the website is easy to use an userfriendly for several usergroups. We did not create a back-log for this project. This would have been nice during the last weeks of the project, and could have made it easier to assign tasks.
