purpose and audience

The purpose of the project for Andres’ Bananas! food trucks, is to help Andrés business to grow by giving him a website that's gripping and clear. To promote his brand and provide possible customers with enough information to know what his food truck provides, its history and how to order food from his food truck.

Therefore, this site will provide the user with a menu, an option for ordering food and the location of the food trucks. There will also be information about Colombian food and history.

Seeing how there are two trucks, located at Gløshaugen and the second alternating between Kalvskinnet, Tunga and Dragvoll, the audience will mainly be students and employees of NTNU.

The Design

Navigation structure

It is possible to access every startingpage from all the pages through a navigation bar. This means they can access the menu, about us and location through a navigation bar. This is to give the user freedom to explore the website and always have an exit form the page they are on.

Page layout and apperance

The idea is to have a Colombian inspired website through the use of traditional colombian colors i.e. the ones from the Colombian flag. The backgroundcolor is yellow and has the same RGB value as the yellow in the flag. The design will be simplistic so the user experience is simple and easy.

  • Style
  • Font: Barlow (fallback Arial)
  • Cholor scheme:
    • Body: #FCD116
    • Order-button: 0085FF
    • Text: #000000
    • Text Order-button: #FFFFFF
  • Body width: 1440 px
  • Links:
    • The logo will be a clikable link that takes user to the homepage.
    • the navbar will have links to: menu, about and live position
    • On hover the font size will expand a few pixles
  • Navbar and style: The navbar will be at the top of the page, three options to the right: menu, about and live position. On the top left will be the websites logo which functions as a link to the homepage.
  • Logo: The logo will be in the top left corner for the desktop version and also be the favicon
  • Footer: the footer will contain contact information with a phone number and email addresss for Andrés Bananas!

Content

  1. Homepage (homepage.html)

    The homepage is simple with the logo, a picture and an “order now” button that takes the user to the menu. Also there is a navigtionbar at the top of the homepage that leads to the other pages.

  2. About (about.html)

    The “about” page is just an overview of Andrés’ history and the reason for opening Andrés Bananas!

  3. Location (location.html)

    On this page there will be two pictures containg the live position of the food trucks and also a schedule for the different days they are on each campus.

  4. Menu (menu.html)

    The menupage has an overview of the different food options at Andrés Bananas! Along with information regarding allergies and price. Also it is the page for ordering food, so when a user chooses a dish it will be included in the “your order” section next to the menu. With the option to discard the choice, see the price and quantity and go forward with the order.

  5. Confirm and pay (payment.html)

    Here the user will get the same overview of their order on the right, and some options on the left. They have to write in their name and choose their preferred method of payment.

  6. Order summary (confirmation.html)

    After the payment is done the user gets feedback telling them the order is being made, with a summary of the order, where to collect it and how long it is until it is finished.

Minimum requirements

The webiste will have four distinct javascript applications to fulfill the minumum requirements

Pål and Herman will be in charge of implementing

  1. A javascript function that will update the position of the trucks depending on which day it is. Seeing how one of the trucks moves during the week it is important to always show its live position
  2. An overlay for the different dishes in the menu. When the user clicks on a dish an overlay will show with different options such as adding ingredients or choosing a vegetarian/vegan option.

Marius and Ståle will be in charge of implementing

  1. a timer that counts down after a user has ordered food. I.e. "the food will be done in "X-minutes""
  2. An a javascript function that keeps track of the dishes the user has chosen. After adding patacones to the cart, a dynamic overview of the users order will be displayed next to the menu. Here the users will have the option to add another or remove the order.
